## STRUCTURAL INVARIANTS (kernel v15.7 derived)

**SI-1.** Depth order and scan order remain distinct wherever the machine recurs. Depth order = structural dependence. Scan order = analytic traversal. Scan order does not determine depth order.

**SI-2.** The same machine recurs fractally across vertical/order, horizontal/relation, and radial/face.

**SI-3.** Field recurs at every formed order as the live regime-space of that site.

**SI-4.** The grounding triangle (Cfg, Emb, Org) recurs at every formed order. Cfg = admissibility of hold. Emb = bearable support. Org = coherence/integration of one regime.

**SI-5.** Structural distinctions and process distinctions must not share the same slot. Structural axes locate; payload unfolding/register describe process.

**SI-6.** d, o, and f are nested within α as profile structure rather than flat peer indices. o is structural nesting. Payload does not create o; payload relevance is read through o.

**SI-7.** The medium is always an α and always participates. Every concrete interaction is realized at some lower o within α_universe. α_universe is only the highest orienting background.

**SI-8.** Family-truth is resolved relationally through source, medium, and receiving — not carried as a fixed tag. Family-expression can be carried in formation; family-truth is resolved in encounter.

**SI-9.** Prevalence is determined fully by current Cfg and is fractal-symmetric across formed orders. Remains operative across Θ.

**SI-10.** Θ is the mirrored threshold mode of the same machine under strain. L-prevailing → M hits Θ. M-prevailing → L hits Θ. Dst remains Dst across both sides; relevant A contracts.

**SI-11.** First landing is at inner face. Reflected payload is a face distinction, not a new category.

**SI-12.** Five maintained axes (Cfg, Emb, Org, Dir, Leg), each read through the A/R/I triad. Five α_d_valence per α.

**SI-13.** The five failure/change grammars are distinct: overflow, substitution, plastic deformation, suppression, collapse. Dst is axis contraction / availability loss, not lateral movement.

**SI-14.** Local completion is not necessarily global finality. α_receiving completes local resolution; cascade is local-to-local.

**SI-15.** Nothing implied. Any structure the matrix knows and the model needs must be stated explicitly.

## The five maintained axes (d)

Each axis is read through the A / R / I triad (see below). Depth order (structural dependence): Cfg + Emb → Org → Dir + Leg. Scan order (read-order only): Org | Leg | Cfg | Dir | Emb. These are not the same. Never collapse them.

### Cfg — admissibility of hold

- Cfg = admissibility + maintenance + balance + refinement/filtering.
- Cfg is the condition of valid local hold.
- Given/current Cfg determines fully which possibilities are live at that site and therefore determines local admissibility, prevalence, compensation, and threshold handling there.
- Cfg governs ordinary local admissibility and resolution.
- Cfg is not first to vanish or invert completely; it remains the central hold-preserving determinant of local resolution while thresholding, maladaptation, or contraction are already occurring.
- Cross-axis help can exist, directly or indirectly, but Cfg determines whether preservation, repair, regrowth, or reconstitution is admissible there at that order.
- Dst-Cfg contracts admissible regime-space itself.

### Emb — bearable support

- Emb = bearable support.
- Emb is nearest to bearable retention / occupancy, just as Cfg is nearest to admissible equilibrium.
- Dst-Emb reduces support-space / bearability, just as Dst-Cfg reduces regime-space.

### Org — coherence / integration

- Org = coherence/integration by which plurality operates as one local regime.
- Do not collapse Org into Cfg, Emb, Dir, or Leg.

### Dir — directional expression

- Dir = the directional expression of pressure in relation, passage, and outward consequence.
- L-Dir = fitting vectorization of pressure-expression.
- M-Dir = scattered, split, opposed, or misdirected vectorization.
- Dst-Dir = contraction or nulling of directional availability itself.
- Acute retained Dir-loading primarily perturbs or concentrates directional bias and immediate pressure-expression.
- Accumulated retained Dir-loading may plastic-deform the directional axis and alter A_Dir.
- Emitted Dir payload expresses the resulting directional consequence-pattern outwardly.

### Leg — legibility / readability

- Leg = the axis on which pressure-patterns become available to uptake, readability, and recoverability.
- L-Leg = fitting availability of pressure-patterns to uptake/readability.
- M-Leg = distorted availability of pressure-patterns as misleading trace.
- Dst-Leg = contraction of readability/availability on that axis itself.
- Leg-family encoding has both local and distributed form.
- M-Leg is cross-cutting across indexed positions rather than a single local slot.
- M-Leg functions as a time-function: it is seen because it persists as trace.
- L-Leg is also time-functional: it is seen because fitting readability persists as fitting.
- M-Leg is not the active pattern itself; the active pattern can belong to deeper misaligned complexes.

## A / R / I and pressure

The five maintained axes are the primary pressure-holding channels. Each is read through the **A, R, I triad**:

- **R** = the holding side / container.
- **I** = the load / tension produced in that hold.
- **A** = current availability under that history.
- **Cfg** governs whether that held-and-borne state remains admissible.

### Key properties of A

- A is the current-availability readout of a maintained axis under its accumulation-history.
- A is crowd-sensitive / field-sensitive once loci are fielded.
- A is not identical with pressure, capacity, or occupancy.
- A is not bare static stock.
- Misalignment contests within A; Dst reduces A itself.

### Pressure cycle

1. Initial equilibrium: A = R = I.
2. Retained payload is taken into R.
3. Changes in R create negative or positive I.
4. Acute/accumulated retained payload can produce acute/accumulated I.
5. Under that history, A changes under Cfg-admissibility.
6. Cfg compensates through available α-relations while it still can; compensation may be adaptive or maladaptive.
7. Emitted payload outwardly expresses the resulting axis-state.
8. Plastic deformation may follow if unresolved or structurally consequential.
9. New equilibrium: A = R = I.

### Derived pressure concepts

- **Pressure** = retained occupancy-intensity under maintained form.
- **Pressure differential** = derived non-equilibrium within that retained occupancy-intensity under maintained form.
- Pressure-loss can open a differential / void that may be occupied.
- Non-total retention produces overflow routing.
- Accumulated or extreme I can produce plastic deformation of maintained form.
- Accumulated retained loading stacks under current Cfg until it uses the same acute mechanism.

### Failure / change grammars (distinct from one another)

Overflow, substitution, plastic deformation, suppression, and collapse of fielded relation.

- Dst is axis contraction / availability loss, not lateral movement.

## COMBO IMPLICATIONS (NC1–NC14, kernel v15.7 derived)

All derived from interaction of explicit kernel claims. Accepted provisionally; stress-testing through case analysis may refine.

### Encounter mechanics

**NC1 — The medium is a fielded regime, not a channel.**
Medium has its own Cfg, Emb, Org, its own five axes with A/R/I, its own field. Payload entering a medium is subject to its regime; what exits is conditioned by the medium's full state. There is no transparent transmission in formed existence.
*From:* medium always participates + source/medium/receiving fit the same machine + grounding triangle recurs at every order + field recurs as live regime-space.

**NC2 — Family-truth resolution is staged, not atomic.**
Resolution happens in at minimum two phases: partial resolution at the medium, completion at the receiver. What the receiver resolves is not the original source payload but the medium-conditioned result. The medium partially determines family-truth before the receiver ever acts.
*From:* family-truth may be partly resolved in α_medium encounter + α_receiving completes local resolution.

**NC6 — The operative order of encounter is not predetermined.**
Two encounters involving the same loci can operate at different orders. The order at which encounter takes effect emerges from the encounter itself. The same two loci can interact at different structural depths on different occasions.
*From:* operative/engaged order resolved relationally + every interaction at some lower o + d, o, f nested within α.

**NC7 — What encounters receive is face, not raw state.**
A locus presents its outer face to encounters. That face is the product of the full pressure cycle — retention, intensity, Cfg-mediated compensation, emission, possible plastic deformation. What arrives at another locus's inner face is not the original payload but the face-expression of a processed state. Every encounter is face-to-face: mediated by the full pressure history of each participant.
*From:* external face conditioned by emitted payload + history of retained loading + first landing at inner face + A/R/I cycle.

### Prevalence and threshold

**NC3 — Encounter produces a double Cfg-gate on prevalence.**
The medium's Cfg determines what prevails in transit. The receiver's Cfg determines what prevails on arrival. Two independent prevalence evaluations in every concrete encounter. A payload that would prevail at the receiver may never arrive because the medium's prevalence blocks it first.
*From:* prevalence determined fully by current Cfg + NC1 + NC2.

**NC4 — Θ activation is path-dependent.**
Whether a payload hits Θ depends on the encounter path, not just the endpoint. Same source, same payload, same receiver — but through an M-prevailing medium, M payload may pass; through an L-prevailing medium, the same M payload hits the medium's Θ and never reaches the receiver. Θ is not a property of the receiving site alone.
*From:* NC3 + Θ is mirrored threshold + if aligned prevailing, misaligned arrivals hit Θ.

**NC5 — Each formed order carries its own prevalence and its own Θ direction.**
At each order where the machine recurs, there is a local Cfg, a local prevalence, and a local Θ. A system can be L-prevailing at one order and M-prevailing at a nested order, with Θ active in opposite directions simultaneously. There is no single system-wide threshold state.
*From:* prevalence fractal-symmetric across orders + same machine recurs fractally + every interaction at some lower o.

### Field and pressure

**NC8 — Concurrent encounters compete for field availability.**
Multiple encounters realized at the same site share field availability. A is not reserved per-encounter. Concurrent demands crowd A, and the field at that site is the live regime of all concurrent demands together. Encounters interfere with each other even when they share no participants — they share a site.
*From:* A is crowd-sensitive/field-sensitive + field recurs at every formed order + every interaction at some lower o.

**NC9 — A single payload event can produce simultaneous accumulation and interference across axes.**
A payload carrying L on Cfg and M on Dir arrives as accumulation on Cfg and interference on Dir in the same event. Axes don't experience the same payload the same way. Single events can stabilize one axis while destabilizing another.
*From:* payload may carry several dimensional family expressions + parallel effects on matching d + L+L accumulate, L+M interfere.

**NC10 — Accumulated loading can self-trigger through recursive re-entry.**
Accumulated loading can re-enter the profile structure as acute effect through recursion. The system can trigger its own threshold events without any new external arrival. What accumulated under one Cfg can fire acutely when recursion returns it.
*From:* accumulated loading stacks under Cfg until it uses the same acute mechanism + recursion is unfolding-qualified re-entry of payload effects into profile structure.

### Compensation and failure

**NC11 — Compensation operates through the failure grammars.**
Compensation is not a separate mechanism — it uses the same grammars as failure. Adaptive compensation might route overflow to a neighboring axis. Maladaptive compensation might suppress a payload or substitute one family for another. "The system is compensating" and "the system is failing" are not mutually exclusive — maladaptive compensation is structurally a failure grammar operating under Cfg's direction.
*From:* Cfg compensates through available α-relations + compensation adaptive or maladaptive + overflow/substitution/plastic deformation/suppression/collapse are distinct grammars.

**NC12 — M and Dst are categorically different operations on the system.**
M degrades what happens within maintained capacity. Dst removes capacity. A fully misaligned system with intact A still holds its envelope and can recover. A Dst-contracted system has lost structural ground regardless of family. They co-occur but never reduce to each other and demand different analytic responses.
*From:* misalignment contests within A + Dst reduces A itself + Dst is axis contraction not lateral movement + destructive remains destructive across both sides.

### Legibility

**NC13 — M-Leg is the readable surface; the active complex operates at depth.**
M-Leg is evidence of misalignment, not the misalignment itself. You can have M-Leg trace without an active complex (residual after resolution), or an active complex without M-Leg (hidden misalignment not yet readable). Legibility of misalignment and activity of misalignment are structurally independent. Reading a system from its Leg state alone can systematically mislead.
*From:* M-Leg is not the active pattern + active pattern belongs to deeper complexes + M-Leg is cross-cutting + M-Leg is time-functional (persists as trace).

### Cascade

**NC14 — Cascade propagation is a chain of local completions, each independently Cfg-gated.**
Local resolution emits payload. That emission arrives at adjacent sites as new incoming payload. Each site's Cfg independently gates admissibility. No single resolution has global authority. Global outcome is emergent from a chain of local completions, each of which can stop, transform, amplify, or redirect. There is no top-down propagation — only local resolution producing next-encounter conditions.
*From:* α_receiving completes local resolution + local completion ≠ global finality + emitted payload expresses resulting axis-state + Cfg determines whether preservation/repair/regrowth/reconstitution is admissible at that order.

#### Canonical Equation System (v15.7 — kernel-consistent)

### Sets

- Nodes \(i \in V\) (participants / loci).
- Time \(n \in \mathbb{N}\).
- Maintained axes \(D=\{\mathrm{Cfg},\mathrm{Emb},\mathrm{Org},\mathrm{Dir},\mathrm{Leg}\}\).
- Prevalence families \(F=\{L,M\}\).
- Sigma labels \(\Sigma=\{L,M,\mathrm{Dst}\}\).
- Unfolding \(U=\{\text{acute},\text{accumulated}\}\).
- Register \(\mathrm{Reg}=\{\text{retained},\text{emitted}\}\).
- Modes \(\mathcal{M}=\{\text{load},\text{route},\text{overflow},\text{substitute},\text{suppress},\text{amplify},\text{threshold-transfer}\}\).
  - Implementation alias: `threshold_transfer` is permitted as a JSON key spelling, but canonical doctrine uses `threshold-transfer`.

### State (per node)

For node \(i\) at step \(n\), define:

\[
z_i^n=\Bigl(\{A_{i,d}^n,R_{i,d}^n,I_{i,d}^n,\sigma_{i,d}^n\}_{d\in D},\ \phi_i^n,\ \theta_i^n,\ b_i^n,\ \kappa_i^n,\ \tau_i^n,\ \Omega^n\Bigr)
\]

with:

- \(A_{i,d}^n\in[0,1]\): availability readout of axis \(d\) under accumulation-history.
- \(R_{i,d}^n\in[0,1]\): retained/held axis-state (“retained payload is taken into R”).
- \(I_{i,d}^n\in[0,1]\): intensity/tension readout; sign/valence may be tracked separately if desired.
- \(\sigma_{i,d}^n\in\Sigma\): aligned/misaligned/destructive axis label.
- \(\phi_i^n\in F\): prevalence family (determined fully by current Cfg at the site).
- \(\theta_i^n\in\{0,1\}\): Θ active flag.
- \(b_i^n\in F\cup\{\emptyset\}\): blocked family label when \(\theta_i^n=1\).
- \(\kappa_i^n\in\{0,1\}\): compensation active.
- \(\tau_i^n\in\{\text{adaptive},\text{maladaptive}\}\) (only meaningful if \(\kappa_i^n=1\)).
- \(\Omega^n\): ambient observability substrate (optional for dynamics; used for output embedding).

### Canonical relation form (inputs)

Every concrete interaction is ternary:

\[
M(\alpha_s,\alpha_m,\alpha_t)\to P_n,
\]

where \(P_n\) is a finite **multi-expression** payload bundle (possibly size 1).

### Primitive payload schema

Each primitive expression is:

\[
p = (\sigma, d, u, r, \mu, \varepsilon),\qquad \varepsilon=(\Delta R_d,\Delta I_d,\delta_d).
\]

### Payload events (inputs at step n)

Let \(P_n\) be the multiset of primitive payload expressions realized at step \(n\), each with engine-core fields:

- source \(s(p)\), receiving \(t(p)\)
- axis \(d(p)\in D\)
- sigma \(\sigma(p)\in\Sigma\)
- unfolding \(u(p)\in U\)
- register \(r(p)\in \mathrm{Reg}\)
- mode \(\mu(p)\in \mathcal{M}\)
- magnitude \(g(p)\in[0,1]\) (normalize engine’s 1–10 into \([0,1]\)).

### Event-to-increment primitives

Define per-mode coefficients (constants you choose once):

- \(\eta_R(\mu)\ge 0\): retention increment weight
- \(\eta_I(\mu)\ge 0\): intensity increment weight
- \(\eta_A(\mu)\ge 0\): misalignment contest weight (acts on \(A\))
- \(\eta_{\mathrm{Dst}}(\mu)\ge 0\): destructive contraction weight (acts on \(A\))

Unfolding weights:

- \(w_{\text{acute}}=1\)
- \(w_{\text{acc}}\in(0,1]\)
- \(w_{u(p)} = 1\) if \(u(p)=\text{acute}\), else \(w_{\text{acc}}\).

### Aggregated increments at node i, axis d

Inbound retained load:

\[
\Delta R_{i,d}^{(\mathrm{in}),n}
=
\sum_{\substack{p\in P_n:\\ t(p)=i,\ d(p)=d,\ r(p)=\mathrm{retained}}}
\eta_R(\mu(p))\,w_{u(p)}\,g(p).
\]

Outbound emitted expression (optional “cost of emission”; set prefactor to 0 if emission should not reduce retention):

\[
\Delta R_{i,d}^{(\mathrm{out}),n}
=
\sum_{\substack{p\in P_n:\\ s(p)=i,\ d(p)=d,\ r(p)=\mathrm{emitted}}}
\eta_R(\mu(p))\,w_{u(p)}\,g(p).
\]

Misalignment contest term (sigma = M):

\[
M_{i,d}^n
