## A / R / I TRIAD

For every maintained axis d:

- **A_d** = current availability under accumulation-history
- **R_d** = retention / holding side
- **I_d** = intensity / tension produced in that hold
- A is not bare static stock; it is current-availability readout under accumulation-history
- A is crowd-sensitive / field-sensitive once loci are fielded
- A is not identical with pressure, capacity, or occupancy

**Hard rule (family impact on availability):**

- Misalignment (M) **contests within** A (degrades what happens within maintained capacity).
- Destructive/ruin (Dst) **contracts A itself** (removes capacity).
- These two operations on A are categorically different and must not be collapsed.
- Cfg gates admissible relaxation / reconfiguration, but **does not gate** the direct contest/contraction effects above.

**Equilibrium cycle:**

1. Initial equilibrium: A = R = I
2. Retained payload is taken into R
3. Changes in R create negative or positive I
4. Acute/accumulated retained payload can produce acute/accumulated I
5. Under that history, A changes under Cfg-admissibility (resolution/relaxation is Cfg-gated)
6. Cfg compensates through available α-relations while it still can
7. Compensation may be adaptive or maladaptive
8. Emitted payload outwardly expresses the resulting axis-state
9. Plastic deformation may follow if unresolved or structurally consequential
10. New equilibrium: A = R = I

**Additional dynamics:**

- Pressure-loss can open a differential / void that may be occupied
- Non-total retention produces overflow routing
- Accumulated or extreme I can produce plastic deformation of maintained form
- Accumulated retained loading stacks under current Cfg until it uses the same acute mechanism

**Signatures:** L (aligned/fitting), M (misaligned/non-fitting), Dst (destructive/ruin — contracts A)

- Alignment / Misalignment / Destruction name whole family-truths, not only accumulated forms
- Misalignment is the analytic term; do not use "corruption"
- Destructive/ruin is the analytic term; do not use "damage"

**Payload accumulation and interference:**

- Aligned payloads accumulate with aligned payloads
- Misaligned payloads accumulate with misaligned payloads
- Aligned and misaligned payloads interfere
- Destructive payloads damp/cancel by acting on axis-potential

**Prevalence:**

- Prevalence is determined fully by current Cfg
- Prevalence itself is fractal-symmetric across formed orders and remains operative across Θ

## PAYLOAD

Payload belongs to the relation-event layer; acts on nested α structure; not part of static profile coordinates.

**Unfolding** ∈ {acute, accumulated}  
**Register** ∈ {retained, emitted}  
Emission is the emitted payload register, not a peer of acute/accumulated.

- Payload may carry one or several dimensional family expressions
- Several expressions arrive as parallel effects on matching d
- First landing is at inner face
- Reflected payload is not a new category; it is a face distinction in the same process

**payload_bearing** = the source-formed bearing/carrying character of payload, resolved relationally in encounter. Remains distinct from receiving α_d_valence even while being resolved relationally.

**Recursion** = unfolding-qualified re-entry of payload effects into profile structure. Not a new axis, not a synonym for accumulation.

### Exact payload schema (kernel-canonical)

p = (σ, d, unfolding, register, μ, ε)

- σ ∈ {L, M, Dst}
- d ∈ {Cfg, Emb, Org, Dir, Leg}
- unfolding ∈ {acute, accumulated}
- register ∈ {retained, emitted}
- μ ∈ {load, route, overflow, substitute, suppress, amplify, threshold-transfer}
- ε = (ΔR_d, ΔI_d, δ_d) = pressure-expression bundle
  - ΔR_d: retention increment contribution (landing into R)
  - ΔI_d: intensity increment contribution (tension shift)
  - δ_d: availability impulse (contest/contraction / opening)

**Multi-expression rule:** a single concrete encounter may deliver a bundle P = {p_i} of one or more primitive expressions.

## Cfg — EXPANDED FORMULATION

- Cfg = admissibility + maintenance + balance + refinement/filtering
- Cfg is the condition of valid local hold
- Given/current Cfg determines fully which possibilities are live at that site: local admissibility, prevalence, compensation, and threshold handling
- R holds; I loads; Emb bears; A measures current availability; Cfg governs whether that held-and-borne state still counts as a stable local regime
- Cfg governs ordinary local admissibility and resolution
- Cfg is not first to vanish or invert completely; it remains the central hold-preserving determinant while thresholding, maladaptation, or contraction are already occurring
- Cross-axis help can exist, directly or indirectly, but Cfg determines whether preservation, repair, regrowth, or reconstitution is admissible there at that order
- Dst-Cfg contracts admissible regime-space itself

## AXIS FORMULATIONS

**Emb:** bearable support. Nearest to bearable retention/occupancy. Dst-Emb reduces support-space/bearability.

**Org:** coherence/integration by which plurality operates as one local regime. Do not collapse into Cfg, Emb, Dir, or Leg.

**Dir:** directional expression of pressure.

- L-Dir = fitting vectorization of pressure-expression
- M-Dir = scattered, split, opposed, or misdirected vectorization
- Dst-Dir = contraction or nulling of directional availability itself
- Acute retained Dir-loading perturbs or concentrates directional bias and immediate pressure-expression
- Accumulated retained Dir-loading may plastic-deform the directional axis and alter A_Dir
- Emitted Dir payload expresses the resulting directional consequence-pattern outwardly

**Leg:** readability of pressure-patterns.

- L-Leg = fitting availability to uptake/readability
- M-Leg = distorted availability as misleading trace
- Dst-Leg = contraction of readability/availability itself
- M-Leg is cross-cutting across indexed positions, not a single local slot
- Both L-Leg and M-Leg are time-functional: persist as trace
- Leg-family encoding has both local and distributed form
- M-Leg is not the active pattern itself; the active pattern can belong to deeper complexes

1. Identify all participants (2–6 typical), assign α addresses with ℓ, o, f
2. Produce 3–8 timesteps. Each timestep: all 5 axes per participant with A/R/I values 0–100, σ family, and α_d_valence. Validate A/R/I per axis before outputting.
3. Include `prevalence` per participant: which family prevails under current Cfg
4. Include `theta` when threshold mode is active: which side hits Θ

7. Include `pressure_dynamics` when differentials, voids, overflow routing are salient
8. Include `analysis` block with: cascade_type, starting_axis, failure_grammar, recovery_type, invariants_tested, key_finding
9. Always include `system_name`
10. Output the full JSON in a code block, then a tappable link
