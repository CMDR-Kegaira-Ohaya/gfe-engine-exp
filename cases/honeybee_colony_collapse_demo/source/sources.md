# Sources — honeybee colony collapse disorder (demo case)

This file is the citations base for the rebuilt demo case.

## Source ledger

### [HB-01]
**US EPA. *Colony Collapse Disorder.*** Updated September 11, 2025.  
Public page defining CCD as the disappearance of the majority of worker bees while the queen, food, and a few nurse bees remain.  
Supports: core descriptive pattern; queen/food/immature-bee persistence.  
Reference: https://www.epa.gov/pollinator-protection/colony-collapse-disorder

### [HB-02]
**USDA Agricultural Research Service. *Colony Collapse Disorder: An Incomplete Puzzle.*** AgResearch Magazine, July 2012.  
Describes the defining characteristic of CCD as the disappearance of most or all adult honey bees, leaving honey and brood but no dead bee bodies.  
Supports: worker disappearance; brood and food remaining; lack of dead bees near the hive.  
Reference: https://agresearchmag.ars.usda.gov/2012/jul/colony

### [HB-03]
**USDA Agricultural Research Service. *Colony Collapse Disorder Progress Report.*** 2010.  
Lists the symptom cluster used in early CCD work, including rapid loss of adult workers, lack of dead workers in and around the hive, and delayed robbing / delayed pest invasion.  
Supports: descriptive symptom bundle; delayed robbing/pest pressure lag.  
Reference: https://www.ars.usda.gov/ARSUserFiles/oc/br/ccd/ccdprogressreport2010.pdf

### [HB-04]
**USDA and US EPA. *USDA and EPA Release New Report on Honey Bee Health.*** May 2, 2013.  
Official summary stating that multiple factors are involved in honey bee colony declines, including parasites and disease, genetics, poor nutrition, and pesticide exposure.  
Supports: multi-stressor field; no single-cause framing.  
Reference: https://www.epa.gov/archive/epapages/newsroom_archive/newsreleases/e04602a5e7aa060685257b5f004a12d3.html

### [HB-05]
**vanEngelsdorp D, Evans JD, Saegerman C, Mullin C, Haubruge E, et al. *Colony Collapse Disorder: A Descriptive Study.** PLOS ONE 4(8): e6481, 2009.**  
Early descriptive study concluding that CCD likely involves interaction between pathogens and other stress factors, and that afflicted colonies differ from control colonies in patterned ways.  
Supports: multi-factor interpretation; descriptive research baseline.  
Reference: https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0006481
