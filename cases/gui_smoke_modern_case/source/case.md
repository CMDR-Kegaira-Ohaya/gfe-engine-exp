# Workbench fixture — quiet room transition

> Provenance: `fixture`
>
> This case is intentionally hand-authored to exercise the product UI. It is **not** solver-certified and should not be read as a solved real-world case.

A city library is reconsidering a dependable quiet reading room after several seasons of weak attendance in other programming areas. Leadership sees the room as one of the few flexible spaces that could host visible collaboration blocks, youth workshops, and public-facing activity. Regular readers, neighborhood partners, and facilities staff do not read the room as “unused.” They read it as a stable public function whose value comes from reliability, concentration, and repeat trust.

The fixture is designed to keep the same participants present across several moments so the workbench has something structural to read:

- `library_leadership`
- `quiet_room_regulars`
- `youth_program_team`
- `facilities_staff`
- `neighborhood_partners`
- `city_budget_office`

## What the fixture is trying to show

The UI job here is not to settle a policy debate. The UI job is to give the workbench enough durable structure that each panel has something meaningful to display.

So the case includes:

- a clear baseline moment
- a funding/review trigger
- a proposal that raises visible public value
- a facilities constraint that reshapes the field
- a legitimacy challenge from neighborhood testimony
- a hybrid draft
- a phased adoption moment

It also includes payload across the canon rack so the registry does not look empty. The fixture deliberately uses `Cfg`, `Emb`, `Org`, `Dir`, and `Leg` so filters, trace, and future entity-first views all have material to work with.

## Focal questions

- When a public institution sees “unused capacity,” what if the people who depend on the space experience it instead as a dependable public good?
- How should visible activation be weighed against quiet but repeat use?
- When funding pressure asks for measurability, where does legitimacy move?
- What kind of hybrid arrangement actually preserves trust rather than merely delaying loss?

## Reading posture

This fixture should be read as a structured rehearsal space for the product:

1. **Case level** — provenance, structure status, artifact presence.
2. **Moment level** — how the field changes step by step.
3. **Participant level** — who is persistent, who absorbs pressure, who gains leverage.
4. **Payload level** — which axis is carrying the pressure in each moment.
5. **Trace level** — how one selected thing propagates across time and relation.

Nothing here should be mistaken for solver-certified structure. The point is to give the interface a disciplined, provenance-explicit fixture rich enough to test the workbench well.
